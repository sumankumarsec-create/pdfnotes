Notes worth keeping.

Published 24 July 2026 · by the person who maintains this site

Every note on this site started life somewhere else — a margin note during a test run, a half-finished doc written at 11pm before an interview, a PDF I'd emailed myself so I wouldn't lose it. This is the story of why they ended up here instead of staying scattered.

The problem with "just take notes"

I work in software testing and automation. Between REST Assured frameworks, CI/CD pipelines, and the usual cycle of interview prep, I'd been collecting notes for years — in whichever app was convenient that week. The result was predictable: notes split across three tools, half of them behind a login I'd forgotten, none of them easy to hand to someone else without exporting, converting, or apologizing for the formatting.

When I looked for existing sites to just read notes like these, most had the opposite problem. Sign up first. Sit through an interstitial ad before the content loads. Scroll past a wall of unrelated "recommended" links to find the one thing you searched for. None of that is necessary to hand someone a PDF.

What I built instead

NOTES. is deliberately plain: black text, white background, one static page, no account system. Every note previews directly in the browser and downloads as a PDF with one click. No email required, no "sign in to continue," no algorithmic feed deciding what you see first — just a search box, a few category filters, and the notes themselves.

It's a static site, which is also a promise of sorts: nothing here depends on a backend that might fall over, and there's no database of user activity to worry about, because none is kept. What you see when you visit is the whole thing.

What's in it, and how it grows

The archive started with the material closest to what I do day-to-day — a REST Assured API automation framework, cloud security fundamentals, Jenkins CI/CD for QA — and has grown outward from there into core computer science (data structures, algorithms) and general interview preparation. Every note is added by hand: written for the site directly, or cleaned up from my own study material and credited where it originated elsewhere. Nothing here is scraped or auto-generated, which is also why growth is steady rather than instant.

Why "worth keeping"

The name is the point. A lot of what gets written down while studying or preparing for interviews is genuinely disposable — useful for a day, then forgotten. The notes that make it onto this site are the ones I kept coming back to myself: dense enough to be useful months later, clear enough that someone with less context than me can still follow them. That's the bar for what gets added, and it's the reason the site grows slowly instead of trying to cover everything at once.

What's next

More subjects, more depth in the ones already here, and — eventually — more posts like this one, when there's something worth saying about how the archive is changing. If you spot something worth fixing, crediting differently, or adding, the contact page reaches the same one person who builds everything else here.
